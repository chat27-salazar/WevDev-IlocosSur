Historical Background
Founded in 1572 by Juan de Salcedo, Vigan was the third-largest Spanish colonial city in the Philippines. Its streets have remained virtually unchanged for over 450 years, earning it UNESCO World Heritage status in 1999.
🏛️
Cultural Significance
Vigan represents the only planned Spanish colonial city remaining in Asia. Its fusion of Asian building design and construction with European architectural concepts makes it a living museum.
✨ Why Visitors Love This Place
Step back in time on the same cobblestone streets where Spanish colonizers, Chinese merchants, and Ilocano artisans once walked. Ride a kalesa at sunset and taste authentic Ilocano cuisine.
